My focus is primarily in data science, but I love web development, game design, and robotics!

Data Science

I love applying statistical and computer science tools to solve difficult problems. There's so many big problems that need to be solved in the world, and data science allows us to test if solutions actually work or just sound good on paper. This is the main focus of my career, educational, and professional ambitions, which also connects to a wide variety of other fields, such as DevOps, cybersecurity, machine learning, as well as relevant context for any field they work in. Data science requires that it's practitioners be familiar with tools of every trade, to ensure that the models and assumptions we build actually match reality and make sense in the real world.

Game Development and Design

Video games are to me, most interesting when they provide useful insights into real world problems. This most often comes in two ways, either through story or gameplay systems, and what interests me the most is using mechanics to teach the player something. Whether it's city builders, strategy games, or the recent wave of sandbox open world games, all of them use interesting systems to teach players about something bigger than just the game itself. My biggest inspirations for my personal work are simulation games like Cities: Skylines, Victoria 2, and Rimworld and systemic games like Far Cry 2, S.T.A.L.K.E.R, and MGS V.

Robotics and "Hands On" Work

I spent 8 years in FIRST robotics, leading my team to the World Championships twice. In that time, I worked on a variety of projects. I designed two-wheeled assemblies that could raise and lower to match varying terrain. I designed, wired, and coded an ultrasonic field tracking system, that would alert us to opposing robots in our path of travel. I ran a CNC milling machine, mentoring most of my team on it's proper setup and operation. That time gave me a strong desire to always learn different and interesting areas of study, as my wide experience gave me a more complete perspective on the technical problems our team faced.
