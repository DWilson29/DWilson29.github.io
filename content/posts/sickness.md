When I started this year, I had a great deal of plans for the coming year, everything from working on a bigger personal project, focusing on my health, and building stronger personal relationships. Additionally, part of those goals were to contribute here more, which I have unfortunately been having difficulty doing.

The last 4 months have been increasingly difficult for me. I was diagnosed and treated with pneumonia back in October, which thankfully could be treated easily with antibiotics. Unfortunately, what couldn't be treated as easily was healing my lungs from the damage. I had childhood asthma, and while I had largely grown out of it, my lungs were significantly weakened by the infection. This took months of medical appointments to figure out the course to treat it, which took until January.

During this period, I felt very productive. As a software engineer, my physical ability is not really relevant to my day to day life. I had some difficulty walking into work, or lifting large items, but outside of that my output was not significantly affected outside of days I physically could not work.

Engineering is built from the mind, not so much the body. In February I started having horrible headaches, combined with intense light sensitivity and vertigo, seemingly all the time. Another few weeks of investigation, and we *think* we've determined the cause, with a referral to a specialist to confirm. Over that time, I was unable to do much of anything besides lay in bed with my eyes closed.

I struggled significantly more with the second infection over the first, as you can imagine. Indeed, I tried setting a schedule to post blog posts near the 1st of the month, and I could hardly focus on a screen. Thankfully, I'm on the up and *think* I have a treatment course that can manage it better.

My main takeaways from my illnesses are

1. Don't push yourself too hard when recovering. Taking time makes sure that you don't inadvertently make the issue worse.
2. Do push yourself to find your limit near the end of your recovery. After 4 months of health issues, my regular exercise routine was obliterated. For me, I wish I kept some strength training while I was having my lung issues, as I've noticed I'm noticeably weaker.
3. If you can't do, think. I couldn't write code, but I could think about my personal project and decide that some of my original ideas really wouldn't work out.
4. Do try to keep doing things you enjoy. The only good part of my illness has been that I've been able to spend a lot of time with one of my hobbies, watching movies. I had a pretty big backlog of films I wanted to see, and now I just have a large backlog!
